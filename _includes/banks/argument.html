1. Iain Banks' utopia, the Culture, is actually not that radically good.
  2. Since it is anxious about ultimate meaning, dependent on continuing intervention elsewhere. (It doesn't take its hedonic achievements and freedoms seriously enough)
  3. Since it does not offer real agency to one sort of human, the kind who needs to do counterfactual good and to be in charge.
  4. Since its post-humans still experience many distinctive human downsides (including rationalisation of death).
  5. Since despite all the freedom and power every subject has, it is somewhat stagnant.
  6. Since the humans might be a bit domesticated.
  7. Since it seems to have a few security holes.
