A stream of associations rather than an argument. But:

  1. Several stereotypes of my generation (the present under-35s) are: wholesome memes, openness about inadequacy, nostalgia (remakes, rewatching, revivals), and extended adolescence ("neoteny"). If these are in fact distinctive, what explains this?
  2. Irony is maybe pervasive, and youth culture is maybe more contrarian than it is ironic. So to be sincere and positive is contrarian. (Actually mostly there's a strange hybrid of both.)
  3. We're a pessimistic bunch compared to 90s youth. But pessimism could inspire either irony and sincerity: detachment or comfort so meh. Also, thinking that the present is bad maybe causes nostalgia for a supposedly good past.
  4. Another cause of nostalgia is maybe the recent fragmentation of culture. Remakes return us to a time of blockbuster common interests. You can talk to basically anyone about the next redo of Jungle Book; you cannot do this about the latest Glowfic session, or .
  5. Postmodernism is a bad explanation for mass irony. Or anything.
